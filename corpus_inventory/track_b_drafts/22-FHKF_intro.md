- **RQ-first opener mirrors 19-JWW.** Sentence 1 of ¶1 is the broad research question ("How does artificial intelligence (AI) affect firms' product quality and efficiency?"), with the M1B lit-contradiction anchor (mixed evidence) and M2 gap statement following inside the same paragraph. This is the second corpus paper to invert the M1A default opener, strengthening the 19-JWW counter-template pattern.
- **M1C theory-grounded variant via labor economics.** The theoretical anchor (Frey-Osborne 2017 automation-exposure framework + Babina et al. 2020 AI-and-productivity literature) imports from labor economics rather than from accounting theory. This is a corpus-novel M1C source domain; the closer analogue is 26-KLYY's cultural-transmission anchor (also imported theory, but from cultural economics). File this as M1C-via-labor-economics sub-variant.
- **M4 tension paragraph absent.** Like 22-LNS, this paper omits the canonical M4 "however, there are reasons why the opposite may hold" tension paragraph. The cost-benefit framing in ¶7 (Section 2.2) hints at tension between automation gains and labor displacement, but this is presented as a *result* (both quality AND efficiency improve, AND labor declines) rather than as competing predictions. Adding 22-FHKF to the corpus would further weaken the "M4 corpus-unanimous" claim — now 4/6 in the augmented corpus rather than 6/6.
