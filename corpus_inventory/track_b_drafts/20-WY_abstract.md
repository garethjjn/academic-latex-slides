## Commentary
This abstract follows the modern empirical audit template closely. It opens with a clear setup linking publicity to political costs, immediately pivots to a quasi-exogenous design (Hurun Rich List), and states a directional prediction. The main finding is split into two concrete auditor responses: reporting strictness and fee increases. The abstract then unpacks heterogeneity by client wealth legitimacy, showing how auditors substitute between reporting and pricing based on regulatory risk. A second heterogeneity layer highlights firm size and auditor style as moderators. The closer ties the mechanism back to political cost theory. Notably, the abstract avoids dense numeric reporting and focuses on directional and strategic outcomes.
