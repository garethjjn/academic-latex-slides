## Commentary (≤ 5 sentences)

19-Aob is a single-author JAE paper that opens with an inverted Block 1 — RQ in sentence 1, lit-baseline only afterward — placing it in the same RQ-first family as 19-JWW. Its Block 2 deploys an unusually rich three-sided tension (AU 110 reasonable-assurance pro-direction · practitioner-judgment counter · practitioner-measure limitations third hand) before pivoting straight into Block 3 dual-data sourcing, with no formal H-statement (the paper is framed as a measurement-concordance study, not a directional-hypothesis test). Final block carries a contributions opener + two un-numbered contributions (M6 form 5 implications + M6 form 8 novel-measure) + M7 risk-based-inspection limitation + canonical JAE-2019 roadmap, supporting the style_dna §8 claim that the JAE-roadmap convention only softens post-2020.

## Reviewer notes (for human)

- **Single-author "I" rather than "we".** 19-Aob breaks the style_dna §6 first-person-plural default. All annotation cells correctly describe rhetorical function without inheriting the singular voice. Worth flagging as an explicit sub-pattern: single-author audit papers retain "I" in JAE (DeFond / Khurana lineage uses "we" even on single-authored work; Aobdia uses "I"). Suggest adding a "voice variant" note to style_dna §6.
- **No formal H statement.** 19-Aob frames the question as a measurement-concordance study, not a directional-hypothesis test, and omits the "**H1.** ... ceteris paribus" form entirely. Defensible for a methods/concordance paper. Pairs with the same omission in 23-ACN (exploratory paper) — pattern: papers with "concordance"/"economics of"/"what matters for" titles default to RQ-only framing.
- **RQ-first opener.** Sentence 1 of ¶1 states the concordance research question; the M1A lit-baseline anchor follows. Same inversion family as 19-JWW. Two of the three pilot intros (19-JWW, 19-Aob) deploy RQ-first openings; 23-ACN uses the canonical M1A-first. Suggests RQ-first may be a more common variant than the corpus claim of "M1A 4/6 default" allows.
