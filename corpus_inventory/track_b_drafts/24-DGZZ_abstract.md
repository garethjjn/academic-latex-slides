## Commentary

The abstract follows a tight 5-move empirical backbone. It opens with a clear setup identifying a staggered policy shock (IBBEA) and immediately states the research question. The prediction-with-tension move is folded into the setup rather than stated as a formal hypothesis; the tension arises from competing theoretical channels (credit access vs. monitoring substitution). The finding move is direct and unambiguous, reporting a negative relation between deregulation and high-quality auditor demand. Heterogeneity is explicitly flagged as the bridge to mechanism interpretation. The abstract then enumerates four distinct channels, one of which (rent protection by incumbent banks) functions as a cost/dark-side mechanism. The Collectively/Overall closer ties the micro-level auditor choice back to macro-level credit market infrastructure. Notably, the abstract carries ZERO effect-size numbers; it reports directionality and mechanisms only, with no sample sizes, years, or coefficient magnitudes included. This aligns with modern JAE conventions that prioritize causal identification and economic channels over raw statistics in the abstract.
