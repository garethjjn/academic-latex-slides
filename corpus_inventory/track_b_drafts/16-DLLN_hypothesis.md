## Commentary (≤ 5 sentences)

Section 2 follows a hybrid multi-H arc not catalogued in `hypothesis_patterns.md`: §2.1 carries the deferred lit anchor (Rows 1-4, the M1A baseline missing from the intro), and §2.2 runs five sequential mini-arcs (H1-H5) without a shared theory umbrella in the modern 26-KLYY sense. Each mini-arc is compressed (1-3 mechanism paragraphs + a bare-bones bridge + a single-sentence numbered "Hypothesis N." block) and uses pre-2024 conventions throughout: no "in the alternative form" qualifier, no "ceteris paribus", no bold/italic "H1." label (the paper uses "Hypothesis 1." style after 07-DHT). The information-intermediary FOR argument runs through H1-H3; the conflict-of-interest normative tension is not given a dedicated M4 paragraph but is converted into the moderation predictions H4 (auditor quality) and H5 (SOX), an unusual "tension-as-moderator" structural device. No outside-accounting theory is cited (cf. modern Bisin-Verdier / Granovetter / Kahneman-Klein anchoring); the paper anchors instead on the audit-process institutional facts and the parallel shared-intermediary literatures (Agrawal et al.; Cai-Sevilir), consistent with the older lit-first 24-DLWW pattern. The H statements use the comparative "more favorable to X than to Y" form (H2) and the moderation "more pronounced when..." form (H3-H5), foreshadowing the modern multi-H pair-prediction device without yet using the H(a)/H(b) syntax.
