## Commentary (≤ 5 sentences)

16-DLLN's first results block (§3.3) tests Hypothesis 1 (shared auditors raise bid probability) with probit marginal-effects on two auditor-sharing proxies (industry network, broad network). It opens with a compact design paragraph (estimator, DV definition, bottom-up IV construction, Palepu control rationale, industry + year FE) before the main coefficient. Economic significance is reported in three linked forms: the raw marginal effect (4.1%), the one-SD probability change (0.29%), and the percent-of-unconditional-base (9.20%). The second proxy (Auditor Network) replicates the result, and the section immediately pre-empts the auditor-size confound that motivates the Big-N subsample robustness check. This design-then-magnitude-then-confound ordering is the canonical results arc, here applied to a binary M&A outcome.
