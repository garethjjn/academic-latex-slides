## Commentary (≤ 5 sentences)

20-CKMS departs from the conventional Big-N + public-client opener and instead opens inside reputation theory (DeAngelo 1981, Causholli-Knechel 2012 credence-good frame), pivoting to a private-firm broker-dealer setting that lets the authors observe every auditor-client match in a regulated market. Block 1 contains a doubled M2 gap statement: one conditional (litigation-versus-reputation confound) and one direct (Big-4 + public-client sampling limitation in prior work), and the M3 research question is non-canonical in framing three predictions of two-sided matching rather than a single hypothesis. Block 2 mechanism (three implications of reputation reliance) is previewed in the intro and then expanded in §2, with the M4 tension paragraph deferred to the body; this is unusual for the modern JAE template. Block 4 leads directly with the main finding rather than with "Consistent with our hypothesis," reports an F6 literature-benchmark magnitude (client misconduct nearly half as important as client size for matching), and chains three downstream findings (cross-sectional bank/IPO partition, BrokerCheck transparency shock, forward-misconduct prediction) before reaching contributions. The Final block carries four contributions in numbered prose form (First/Second/Third/Last) covering the reputation-litigation separation literature, the universe-coverage methodological asset, the matching-theory bridge, and the BD misconduct corpus.
