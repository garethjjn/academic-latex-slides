### Why the audit-write corpus excludes survey/experimental papers

The audit-write Track B corpus distills the rhetorical structure of empirical-archival audit-quality papers in the DeFond / Zuo / Khurana lineage (JAE / JAR / TAR). Survey and experimental papers (e.g., Hanlon-Shroff 2022 JAE; Glover-Hansen-Seidel 2019 questionnaire studies; Westermann-Cohen-Trompeter 2019 interview studies) follow different intro conventions:

1. **No formal hypothesis / no formal H statement.** 22-HS frames its inquiry as descriptive research questions ("whether, how, and why") rather than as falsifiable directional hypotheses, because survey designs cannot deliver the coefficient + standard-error reportage that Block 4's M5 magnitude moves require.
2. **No Block 4 magnitude-translation move.** The "finding" sentences in 22-HS report response-rate proportions ("78% of the inspectors perceive that auditors 'frequently' or 'very frequently' adjust general audit procedures"), not regression coefficients or standard-deviation shifts. The M5 F1-F6 framing forms in `move_bank.md` do not apply.
3. **No Block 5 identification battery.** Survey data have no quasi-experimental shock, no auditor rotation, no falsification placebo. The 22-HS validation move ("we examine whether inspector responses to the above question are associated with changes in unsigned discretionary accruals") is supplementary archival corroboration, not a Block 5 identification battery in the DeFond-Qi-Si-Zhang 2025 sense.
4. **Different opening conventions.** 22-HS opens on policy motivation ("Over the past two decades, several countries have changed their approach to oversee the auditing profession"), which the canonical intro_patterns.md `Forbidden openings` list explicitly excludes for empirical-archival papers. Survey papers' policy-motivation openings are appropriate to their genre but would mis-train the audit-write skill if added as exemplars.
