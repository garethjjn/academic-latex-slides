# Deck Blueprints

Use these as defaults, not prisons. Adjust only after the interview clarifies the talk.

## Lecture

Default narrative:

1. Title
2. Learning objectives
3. Course roadmap / where this lecture fits
4. Core concept 1
5. Core concept 2
6. Derivation, worked example, or demonstration
7. Exercise, discussion, or application
8. Summary / takeaways

Use when:

- the goal is teaching
- the audience needs scaffolding
- examples and recap matter more than novelty

Typical slide roles:

- concept definition
- intuition
- notation
- derivation
- example
- practice
- summary

## Research talk

Default narrative:

1. Title
2. Motivation
3. Research question / gap
4. Contribution
5. Data and design
6. Main results
7. Supplementary analysis
8. Conclusion

Use when:

- the goal is persuasion around a research contribution
- the audience needs the question, design, and result chain quickly
- novelty and evidence matter more than exhaustive pedagogy

Typical slide roles:

- puzzle / motivation
- literature gap
- hypothesis or conceptual framework
- setting and sample
- empirical design
- main result
- mechanism or heterogeneity
- robustness
- conclusion

## Pacing guidance

- `lecture`: allocate more slides to explanation, worked examples, and recap.
- `research talk`: allocate more slides to motivation, identification, results, and interpretation.
- If timing is short, cut breadth before cutting the narrative spine.
- If the user requests many slides for little content, warn that the deck may feel padded and propose a tighter outline.
